Overview

Docker Learning Hub (available at docker.wystone.tech) is a free, educational platform dedicated to teaching Docker and containerization. This privacy policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and your rights regarding your data.

We are committed to protecting your privacy. Our site does not require user accounts, does not collect personal information through forms, and does not process any purchases. We are transparent about what minimal data we collect and why.

Key Points
No user accounts • No form submissions • No purchases • No personal data collection • Educational content only

What Data We Collect

When you visit Docker Learning Hub, we collect only the following:

1. IP Address

Your IP address is automatically recorded by our CDN provider (Cloudflare) and web server. This information is used for:

  • Detecting and preventing abuse or attacks
  • Approximate location identification (country/region level only)
  • Basic traffic statistics

We do not store your IP address for marketing or tracking purposes.

2. Usage Analytics

We use Google Analytics 4 to understand how visitors use the site. The Google Analytics Measurement ID is G-BMPGZRMLCK. The analytics script is only loaded after you explicitly accept analytics in the site cookie banner; declining prevents the analytics script from loading. Note: Google AdSense is used only to serve ads and is not loaded until you explicitly accept the cookie-consent banner shown on first visit. Your consent choice is stored locally (localStorage key wystone_cookie_consent) and can be changed by clearing your browser storage or using the site banner.

3. Cookies

Our site uses cookies from:

  • Cloudflare: Essential cookies for security and performance
  • Google AdSense: Cookies for ad personalization and serving

4. No Personal Information Collected

We do not collect:

Cookies & Tracking Technologies

A cookie is a small file stored on your device that helps websites remember information about you.

Types of Cookies We Use
Essential Cookies (Required)
  • Cloudflare cookies: Required for security and DDoS protection. Cannot be disabled without affecting site access.
  • Session cookies: Help maintain your session while browsing
Advertising Cookies (Optional)
  • Google AdSense cookies: Enable personalized ads and ad performance tracking
Managing Cookies

You can control cookies in your browser:

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Preferences → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Cookies and website data
  • Edge: Settings → Privacy, search, and services → Clear browsing data

Disabling non-essential cookies will not affect the core functionality of our site, though analytics and ads may not work properly.

Site cookie banner: On first visit you will see a cookie-consent banner allowing you to Accept or Decline advertising and analytics storage. Accepting enables AdSense and analytics storage; declining prevents those scripts from loading. Your choice is stored locally and respected on future visits.

⚙️ Do Not Track
If you have enabled "Do Not Track" in your browser, we will respect this preference. Cloudflare and Google AdSense may have their own policies regarding this setting.

Your Rights Under GDPR

If you are located in the European Union or European Economic Area, you have specific rights regarding your personal data under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

Your GDPR Rights
Right to Access

You can request a copy of the personal data we hold about you. Since we collect minimal data and don't maintain user accounts, this would primarily include any data held by Google AdSense and Cloudflare.

Right to Rectification

You can request correction of inaccurate data. However, since we don't collect direct personal information, this applies mainly to third-party services.

Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten")

You can request deletion of your data. Contact us for assistance, though we recommend:

  • Clear your cookies (which deletes tracking data)
  • Disable JavaScript to prevent third-party tracking
  • Use a VPN to mask your IP address
Right to Data Portability

You can request your data in a portable format. Since most of your data is held by third parties (Google, Cloudflare), contact them directly.

Right to Object

You can object to:

  • Analytics tracking: Opt out via Google Analytics Opt-out
  • Personalized ads: Opt out via Google Ads Settings
  • Cloudflare tracking: Use browser privacy settings or contact us
Right to Restrict Processing

You can request that we limit how we use your data. Contact us to discuss options.

How to Exercise Your Rights

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at the email address in the Contact section below. We will respond to your request within 30 days (as required by GDPR).
